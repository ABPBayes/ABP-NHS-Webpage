About Inclisiran - Brand name: Leqvio

Inclisiran is from a new class of medicines used to treat high blood cholesterol.

Cholesterol is a fatty substance in your blood that can block your blood vessels if levels become too high. This can make you more likely to have heart problems or a stroke.

Inclisiran is specifically for people whose cholesterol is not adequately controlled with first line medications such as statins, ezetimibe, or ezetimibe with bempedoic acid (in those who are statin intolerant).

Inclisiran is available on prescription only, and is given by injection under the skin. After the first dose, the next dose will be given after three months, then you will only need it every six months. It can be prescribed by GPs and other health professionals in primary and secondary care.

  • Clinical trial evidence shows that inclisiran can lower LDL cholesterol levels by at least 50%.
  • Near-maximum reductions in LDL cholesterol levels are seen from 90 days of treatment.
  • Inclisiran is proved to be safe and efficient in the long-term in the latest clinical trials by Novartis. It is generally well tolerated, the most common side effects reported include pain and redness or rash at the injection site.
  • Treatment with Inclisiran is usually for life.
